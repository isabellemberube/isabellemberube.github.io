RESIDENT LIBRARIANS WANTED: Hush House, Brancrug

Seaside Salons and Scholarship Awaits You

The famous Brancroft Manor, colloquially known as "Hush House" is in need of a 13th librarian to care for its impressive collection of over 4,000 "unusual" volumes. Located just across the Curcurbit Bridge from the town of Brancrug, this lonely estate is just the place for eclectics to roost. Whether you're conducting strange rituals by moonlight, or frightening locals with our strange chantings at the pub-- this seaside destination offers the chance to become part of something much, much greater than yourself.

Quick Facts

  • Hush House contains over one hundred rooms within its beachside estate
  • The manor is completely free to any willing to work as the manor's librarian.
  • The nearby city of Brancrug has a lively six residents, with facilities such as a post office, pub, and coffin store.
  • Six guest bedrooms, a Librarian's suite, a prison, and a finished basement (no bathrooms, though; hope that isn't too inconvenient for you.)
  • View the layout using our handy online floorplan.

If you're looking to escape the hustle and bustle, or that gnawing feeling that someone, somewhere, is chasing you, and they won't stop until they finally find you-- Hush House is the reprieve you are looking for. Let the Cornish seaside be your (immortal) life's dedication. To visit Brancrug, board your nearest vessel bound for a terrible shipwreak where you are left as the lone survivor. We also expense Uber.

Hush House is the setting of the indie game "Book of Hours". This game was made by Lottie Bevan and Alexis Kennedy of Weather Factory a game studio from the UK. Image is sourced from their concept art.
